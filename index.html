Design Beginnings


Design Fuego

For my first project, I used a Github template and Visual Studio Code to create a basic webpage about myself and my goals for this course. Two interesting skills I learned were how to make a button linked to a new webpage and how to frame the pictures, like the pink and yellow pictures above!

Logo A/B/C

I designed 3 logos using Canva and Adobe Express templetes to create a brand I thought represented myself. I tried to use a consistent color scheme and rely on the lettering and framework of the pre-generated templetes instead of creating something entirely myself.

UX Testing Logo Project

I conducted UX testing on my logo designs to evaluate their effectiveness and usability. I created a script of questions and interviewed 3 people for feedback so I could makeiterative improvements to enhance the clarity and visual appeal of the logo and create one final design.

Cartoon Portrait

I used Adobe Illustrator to design a cartoon-style portait of myself. I used the pen tool to create shapes and outline an image of myself.

Good Design

I used my new knowledge of design principles to evaluate a concert poster of one of my favorite bands that I thought was an example of good design. I used Adobe Illustrator to display and explain my analysis.

Course Goals


1. Consider the role of rhetoric, technology, and ethics in acts of making.

I learned how controlling the way a user interacts with a design, like what features they look at first, is an important persuasion tool when emphasizing certain text or images. I used technology and AI to streamline my design process instead of starting every project from scratch. I explored how ethical considerations in design can impact user experience and accessibility by creating alt text for images on my Fuego website and using contrast to ensure readability.

2. Leverage Design Principles.

Rather than making aesthetic decisions based solely on preference, I structured compositions to guide viewer attention and clarify meaning. I became more intentional about visual flow and , ensuring that each project had a clear focal point and logical progression. Another example was how I used the repetition of colors in my final logo and my Good Design project to create unity and draw the viewer's attention to the right places.

3. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

I both gave and received constructive feedback in our small group discussions, making sure to build off of my classmate's comments to reach a consensus on how an issue could be improved. Listening to peers helped me see blind spots in my work, while critiquing others imporoved my analytical skills.

4. Practice revision within and across media forms.

This goal was primarily acheived within my logo deisgn, since I received feedback from my 3 interviewees and all fo the peers in my presentation group on what to revise. I liked the detail my group memebrs put into their slides, like inclduing details about the ethos of their participants interview answers and the interests/personality traits of theirs, which was a piece of informtion I went back and added to my presentation immediatly. Another example was for the two-page spead when I was struggling with the background image. I had recreated the curved lined across both pages of the spread, but it did not line up perfectly with the section of the line visible in the background image, and I did not have a copy of the image without the curved line. My peer suggested I use an AI photo editor to remove the line so I would have a blank image to use, which was a simple revision.

5. Learn to present your work.

I learned how to present my work effectively by preparing clear, concise slides for my logo testing project and practicing my delivery. I also learned how to receive feedback gracefully, or to tease out better feedback from either research participants or peers by asking better questions. .

Short Form


Two-page Spread

I recreated this spread from an old Esquire issue using Adobe Indesign. I learned how write on lines and use the Adobe Fonts website to match fonts from the original design. My favorite part was changing the width, length, and spacing of the letters to match the specific text. The hardest part was designing the Esquire logo without the overlapping lines, and getting the "Jeff" lines to curve.
